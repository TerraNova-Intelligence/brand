Brand Identity Guide
Who is TerraNova?
TerraNova consolidates international employment law so HR and legal professionals can ask questions and receive verbatim law alongside a human-readable summary — never legal advice, always sourced truth.
🚧 This is a living document. Sections marked "to refine" need team input before locking for production use.
Brand essence
Authoritative clarity — we surface the truth of the law, precise and accessible, so professionals can act with confidence across borders.
The core promise
Employment law, in plain language, from the source. No guesswork. No paraphrase. No legal advice — just the law and what it means.

Typography Rules
Two families only
DM Serif Display + DM Sans. DM Mono for citations only. Never introduce a third typeface.
Italic is editorial
Use DM Serif italic for display moments only — never for emphasis in body copy.
Minimum 14px body
For accessibility and our legal-professional audience. Never below 12px for any visible UI text.
Generous line-height
Body: 1.75. Dense legal content needs breathing room — never set below 1.5.

Visual Identity
Imagery & Visual Motifs
TerraNova's visual world is grounded, internationally considered, and professional. Photography should convey clarity and confidence, with warm natural tones aligned to the Palladian palette.
🖼 Logo files TBD. Update this section once the final logo is confirmed.
Photography Direction
✓
Real working environments
Offices, laptops, meetings — diverse mid-market professional contexts. People doing actual work, not posed stock.
✓
Geographic diversity as a feature
City skylines, varied faces, maps, subtle global cues. Reinforces the cross-border value proposition.
✓
Warm, natural tones
Photography should be tonally consistent — warm neutrals, natural light, grounded environments aligned with the Palladian palette.
✗
No legal clichés
No gavels, scales of justice, courtrooms, or law books. TerraNova is not a law firm.
✗
No generic stock
No smiling people pointing at whiteboards, fake handshakes, or abstract tech backgrounds.
✗
No cold or high-contrast imagery
Avoid stark white or cool-blue photographic tones — they conflict with the warm palette identity.

Brand Communication
Voice & Tone
TerraNova's voice is consistent — authoritative, clear, and human. Tone shifts by context: precise for results, warm for onboarding, direct for disclaimers.
Voice Attributes
Authoritative, not intimidating
We know the law. We state it plainly — no unnecessary hedging, and never pretending to be legal advice.
Clear, not dumbed down
Our users are smart professionals. Plain language means respecting their time, not assuming ignorance.
Trustworthy, not stiff
We cite sources and admit our limits. Honesty is the foundation of trust in a legal-adjacent product.
Efficient, not terse
Every word earns its place. We don't pad for formality, but give enough detail to act confidently.
Do vs. Don't
✓ Do
"Under the Employment Rights Act 1996 (UK), employees with at least two years' service are entitled to a statutory redundancy payment."
✗ Don't
"Based on our AI's analysis, it seems like UK employees might generally be owed some form of redundancy in many situations."
✓ Do
"TerraNova provides legal information, not legal advice. For decisions with legal consequences, consult a qualified employment lawyer."
✗ Don't
"Disclaimer: Nothing in this platform should be construed as constituting legal advice of any kind whatsoever..."
✓ Do
"We don't have coverage for this jurisdiction yet. Our team is expanding — check back or contact us."
✗ Don't
"Unfortunately at this time we are unable to provide information regarding the requested jurisdiction."
The "Not Legal Advice" Principle
This is one of TerraNova's most important brand signals — consistent, confident, and non-apologetic.
Short form (inline UI)
"TerraNova provides legal information sourced from primary legislation. This is not legal advice."
Full form (footer / onboarding)
"TerraNova consolidates and summarizes publicly available employment legislation. All information is sourced from primary legal texts. TerraNova does not provide legal advice. For decisions with legal consequences, please consult a qualified employment lawyer in the relevant jurisdiction."
